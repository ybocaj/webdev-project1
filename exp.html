Prior Experience

CMA

I graduated from Portland State in 2004, and got my first post-degree job later that year, working as a Support Specialist for Content Management Associates in Oregon City. I was surprised I was hired, because they worked with SQL and I hadn't ever used that, but they taught me and I used SQL daily, writing insert, update, and delete queries to resolve issues. As I got better with joining tables, I wrote billable reports in which I extrapolated data for clients.

I left the Support team and helped the Publishing team by working with the Adobe Suite to build doc types from scratch. After that I moved to the QA team where I got to test the product.

When our largest client left, we were all laid off and the company was ultimately sold, so my takeaway is to ask future employers - during the interview process - if they have multiple revenue streams. But it was lame, because I worked there for nearly 8 years.

Springbrook

My next job was working in downtown Portland for Springbrook as a Support Specialist. Before I got to my 90-day review, I was contacted by an employer that was closer to home.

Axis Clinical Software

I interviewed at Axis and was ultimately hired, which I was happy for, because 3 months of battling rush hour traffic was enough for me. At Axis, I began as a Support Specialist, I remoted into systems, tested patches, assisted QA with regression testing, worked on the AWS platform, and learned to backup and migrate data.

After an associate left the QA team, I was asked to join, which I did. I was responsible for learning about software requirements, and I tested both the electronic and flagship versions of PATS (medical software).

The company was working in the red for a long time, before they decided to cut all their staff, except for a few developers. I hear the plan was to build a more modern product, which is part of what has compelled me to learn. I figure if I learn enough, it'll be that much harder to be laid off in this volatile industry.
